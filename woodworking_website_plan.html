Typography Direction

Headlines

Cormorant Garamond — a refined, high-contrast serif. Gives weight and elegance to piece titles and section headers. Used large, with generous spacing.

DisplaySerif

Body + UI

Inter or DM Sans — a clean, neutral sans-serif. Used for descriptions, navigation, and admin UI. Keeps everything readable without competing with photography.

BodySans-serif
Site Structure / Pages
1

Home

Full-bleed hero image with large serif headline and a single CTA. Below: a curated 3-piece feature section, a brief craftsman statement, and a commission invitation block. Sets the tone entirely.

2

Portfolio (Work)

Masonry or editorial grid of all pieces. Each card shows the hero photo, title, and an Available / Sold badge. Filterable by category (Furniture, Custom Commission). Click opens the piece detail page.

3

Piece Detail

Full-width photography, piece title, materials, description, dimensions, status badge, and price. A prominent "Inquire about this piece" button triggers an inline contact form or scrolls to contact.

4

Commissions

Explains how a custom commission works — the process, timeline, and what to expect. Warm, editorial layout with workshop photography. Ends with an inquiry CTA.

5

About

The craftsman's story — background, philosophy, workshop. Feels personal. One large photo, a few paragraphs. Fully editable from the admin.

6

Contact

Clean inquiry form with fields for name, email, piece of interest (linked or free text), and message. Submissions are visible in the admin panel and sent via email.

A

Admin Studio (private route)

A CMS interface at /studio. Add and edit pieces (photos, title, description, price, status, category). Edit the About page. View contact form submissions.

Scroll Animations
🖼

Hero Parallax

The hero image scrolls at a slower rate than the page, creating depth as the headline lifts away from the photo.

✦

Piece Card Reveal

Portfolio cards fade and slide up as they enter the viewport, staggered slightly so they cascade in from left to right.

—

Section Divider Draw

A thin gold line animates from left to right as a section header enters view, drawing attention before the content follows.

📸

Image Crop Reveal

On piece detail pages, photos reveal themselves with a clipping mask that slides away, like unveiling the work.

✍

Text Fade Up

Body text and descriptions fade in gently from slightly below, so the page feels alive without being distracting.

Recommended Tech Stack

Next.js 14 (App Router)

React framework with server-side rendering, image optimisation built in, and great performance. Industry standard for portfolio sites that need dynamic data.

Sanity.io — CMS + Admin

Gives the owner a polished, hosted admin studio at /studio. Manages pieces, photos, status, categories, and the About page with no code. Free tier is more than enough.

GSAP (ScrollTrigger)

The gold standard for scroll-based animations on the web. Handles all the parallax, reveals, and line draws described above with silky performance.

Tailwind CSS

Utility-first CSS. Fast to style, easy to maintain, and pairs well with Next.js. Custom design tokens for the forest green and gold palette.

Resend — Email (contact forms)

Simple developer-friendly email API. When someone submits the contact form, Resend fires off an email to the owner. Free tier covers hundreds of emails per month.

Vercel — Hosting

One-click deploy from GitHub. Free tier handles this traffic easily. Automatic HTTPS, CDN, and preview deployments for every commit.

Design Principles for This Site

Photography First

The work sells itself. Every layout decision should give photography room to breathe. White or cream backgrounds, generous margins.

Layout

Restrained Color

Forest green and gold are used as accents, not backgrounds. Cream and white dominate so the pieces pop against a neutral canvas.

Color

Slow and Considered

Animations are subtle and purposeful. Nothing bounces or spins. Every motion should feel like the deliberate movement of a craftsman.

Motion
