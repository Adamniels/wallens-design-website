Typography Direction

Headlines

Cormorant Garamond — a refined, high-contrast serif. Gives weight and elegance to collection titles and section headers. Used large, with generous letter spacing. Feels luxurious without being loud.

DisplaySerif

Body + UI

DM Sans or Inter — a clean, neutral sans-serif. Used for descriptions, navigation, pricing, and admin UI. Keeps everything legible without competing with product photography.

BodySans-serif
Site Structure / Pages
1

Home

Full-bleed hero with a large serif headline and a single CTA such as "Explore the Collection". Below: a curated three-piece feature section, a brief designer statement, and a custom order invitation block. Sets the tone of refined luxury from the first scroll.

2

Collection

Editorial grid of all pieces. Each card shows the hero photo, title, material, and an Available or Sold badge. Filterable by category: Rings, Necklaces, Earrings, Bracelets, and Custom. Clicking a card opens the piece detail page.

3

Piece Detail

Full-width photography, piece title, materials, gemstones, description, dimensions, availability status, and price. A prominent "Inquire About This Piece" button leads to an inline contact form. E-commerce add-to-cart functionality will be added in the next phase.

4

Custom Orders

Explains how a bespoke jewelry commission works — the consultation process, materials sourcing, timeline, and what to expect at each stage. Warm, editorial layout. Ends with an inquiry CTA so prospective clients can start the conversation.

5

About

The designer's story — background, philosophy, studio, and craft. Feels personal and unhurried. One large portrait or studio photo, a few paragraphs. Fully editable from the admin without touching code.

6

Contact

Clean inquiry form with fields for name, email, piece of interest (linked or free text), and message. Submissions appear in the admin panel and trigger an email notification to the owner.

A

Admin Studio (private route)

A CMS interface at /studio. Add and edit pieces — photos, title, description, price, materials, gemstones, status, and category. Edit the About page content. View and manage contact form submissions.

E-Commerce Phase Coming Next
S

Shop / Cart / Checkout

In the second phase, the site will gain a full shopping experience. Each piece detail page gets an "Add to Cart" button. A cart drawer slides in from the right. Checkout will be handled via Stripe, keeping the experience seamless without leaving the site.

O

Order Management

Order history and status tracking visible in the admin panel. Inventory management tied to piece availability badges. Email confirmations sent automatically to the customer and the owner on each purchase.

Scroll Animations
🖼

Hero Parallax

The hero image scrolls at a slower rate than the page, creating depth as the headline lifts away from the photo.

✦

Collection Card Reveal

Jewelry cards fade and slide up as they enter the viewport, staggered slightly so they cascade in from left to right — like pieces being laid on a display cloth.

◦

Section Divider Draw

A thin champagne gold line animates from left to right as a section header enters view, drawing attention before the content follows.

📸

Image Crop Reveal

On piece detail pages, photos reveal themselves with a clipping mask that slides away — like lifting a velvet cover from a finished piece.

✍

Text Fade Up

Body text and descriptions fade in gently from slightly below, so the page feels alive and considered without being distracting.

Recommended Tech Stack

Next.js 14 (App Router)

React framework with server-side rendering, image optimisation built in, and great performance out of the box. Industry standard for portfolio and commerce sites that need dynamic data.

Sanity.io — CMS + Admin

Gives the owner a polished, hosted admin studio at /studio. Manages pieces, photos, availability, categories, materials, and the About page content with no code required. Free tier is more than sufficient.

GSAP (ScrollTrigger)

The gold standard for scroll-based animations on the web. Handles all the parallax, reveals, and line draws described above with silky, high-performance motion.

Tailwind CSS

Utility-first CSS. Fast to style, easy to maintain, and pairs naturally with Next.js. Custom design tokens for the ivory, champagne gold, and dusty coral palette.

Stripe — Payments (Phase 2)

The most trusted payment platform for e-commerce. Will power the shopping cart and checkout flow in the second phase. Seamless integration with Next.js via the official Stripe SDK.

Resend — Email (contact forms + order confirmations)

Simple developer-friendly email API. Fires off notifications when someone submits the contact form or places an order. Free tier covers hundreds of emails per month.

Vercel — Hosting

One-click deploy from GitHub. Free tier handles this traffic easily. Automatic HTTPS, global CDN, and preview deployments for every commit.

Design Principles for This Site

Photography First

Fine jewelry lives or dies by its photography. Every layout decision should give product images room to breathe. Ivory and white backgrounds let the pieces take center stage.

Layout

Restrained Luxury

Champagne gold and dusty coral are used as accents, not backgrounds. Sand beige and ivory dominate so the pieces pop against a clean, neutral canvas. Nothing competes with the jewelry itself.

Color

Slow and Considered

Animations are subtle and purposeful. Nothing bounces or spins. Every motion should feel like the unhurried attention of someone handling something precious and rare.
